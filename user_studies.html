How well it really works

User Studies

Our goals:


First, find out if participants naturally rebalance when the shoe tightens. The answer: Absolutely yes! A prime example is Participant 1, whose uncalibrated shoe was constantly actuating. Without knowing anything about the function of the shoe, he naturally responded to the shoe by continuously leaning away from the actuation to the left, so much so that he had to support himself on a nearby shelf!


Second, check reliability. Through each new participant, we discovered something we hadn't done perfectly the first time, and were able to fix it to be more and more reliable.


Third, find actuation sensitivity and shoelace tightness. We were able to develop a method to adjust sensitivity and tightness to each participant such that the product was tailored to their own needs.

Methods:


        Put on shoe


        Stand 5 minutes -- get used to the shoe


        Stand 5 minutes with actuation on, observe how they intiuitively respond


        Inform participant of goal


        Shift weight side to side -- get a feel for actuation


        Sit down and stand up
